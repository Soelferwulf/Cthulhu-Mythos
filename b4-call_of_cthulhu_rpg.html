Well... It's a Tabletop RPG

Call of Cthulhu is Chaosium Inc.’s roleplaying game of mystery and horror inspired by the fiction of H. P. Lovecraft, the originator of cosmic horror. In this game, ordinary people are confronted with the machinations of the Cthulhu Mythos. Set in the 1920s, the modern-day, or beyond, players portray investigators of the unknown—decent folk taking a stand against the terrors of the night despite the costs to their sanity and their lives. Only they can save humanity and the world from unthinkable horror.

Scared yet? Wait till you hear who you're going to play...

Surely some great hero with supernatural powers, right? I'm afraid not, old chap. According to Chaosium: "Call of Cthulhu investigators come from all walks of life, be they journalists, librarians, or school teachers, or even private eyes, taxi drivers, or a hobo. They face mysteries that cannot be explained by rational means. Mysteries riddled with ancient and forgotten lore that humanity was not meant to know."

Source – Chaosium

Kind of... But not really

So if you are still reading this, i will suppose you have already heard about Dungeons & Dragons. If not the mother, surely at least the annoyingly perfect big brother of all Role Playing Games. Bunch of cool people sitting in a room or infront of their webcams, pretending that they are heroic (or not so heroic) characters in a beautiful fantasy world. One of them, the Game Master, is building up the world and leading them through daring (or not so daring) adventures. What happens is completely up to the players and GM, there is a set of rules telling you how to handle certain situations and how to develop your characters as they gain experience and new levels. And there is A LOT of dice. But there is plenty of places to read about D&D so let's focus on Call of Cthulhu again and what are the differences between the two.

Game System

Unlike D&D, Call of Cthulhu doesn't work with levels or even experience. You can't choose what to improve and what new skills you get at the new level, instead you put a tick at every skill that you have succesfully used in a scenario. At the end you make improvement check to see if and how much has the particular skill increased. Skill checks are based on percentile roll, where you have to roll less, than your skill value. Additionally, if your roll was really good, you can get hard or extreme success. You can also be told by the Lore Keeper (that's how GM is called in CoC), that to succeed in a particular situation, you need to pass a hard check. Also, there is one special treat in form of Sanity. Sometimes your characters have to pass a Sanity check, to see how their minds handled particularly creepy situation or something they have glimpsed in the shadows. When they fail, or even when they succeed, depending on the situation, they loose part of their sanity. If that happens too often, the character might get into a bout of madness, temporarily succumbing to various mental conditions, such as amnesia, fear, paralysis and many other and even funnier ones. Eventually you can become temporary insane, getting phobias and so on. If you run out of Sanity, your character becomes permanently insane and is not anymore a player charater. So basically there are two ways to die in CoC. For more details check the Sanity rules and tables on Cthulhuwiki.

Setting

But let's not dwell on rules too much. CoC is a very story driven game. You won't spend as much time on a battle map, as you might in D&D, though that of course varies with every GM and group of players, so take this with a grain of salt. The setting is therefore what really makes the game. In the original version, your adventures take place in 1920s, with all that comes with it, the booming science, that has to figure out much yet, old cars, smoke filled PI offices, hats and prohibition. On this background plays out a game of occult, cosmic entities interfering with human affairs and madness threatening to take over your mind on every corner, while facing things, that ought not to be. As merely human investigator, you can very fast come to be the hunted, instead of the prey. It is a game of mystery and survival, in Lovecraft Country you can barely trust anyone.
